Project Overview

The ELECT Project aims to strengthen higer education and research in energy efficient lighting technologies to support Zambias transition toward clean energy and enviromental Sustainability. ThE Project is coordinated by Aalto University and implemented in partnership with Hellenic Open University, Mulungushi University and Copperbelt University.Co founded by the European Union.

Co-funded by the European Union's Erasmus+ Programme, ELECT focuses on curriculum development, research capacity building, and practical implementation of energy-efficient lighting solutions to support Zambia's national energy conservation goals.

Background & Context

Energy Challenges in Zambia

High Energy Demand

Zambia faces increasing energy demands due to population growth and economic development, particularly in urban areas and industrial sectors.

Lighting Efficiency Gap

Significant potential exists for energy savings through efficient lighting, which accounts for approximately 15-20% of electricity consumption in Zambia.

Skills Development Need

Limited local expertise in energy-efficient lighting technologies and system design creates barriers to widespread adoption and maintenance.

ELECT belongs to the Erasmus+ programmer of the European Union for the action Capacity Building in higher Education (CBHE).

CBHE action supports international cooperation projects based on multilateral partnerships between organizations active in the field of higher education. it supports the relevance, quality, mordenization and responsiveness of higher education in third countries not associated to the programme for socio- economic recovery growth and prosperity.

Purpose

To transform higher education in Zambia by integrating energy-efficient lighting technologies into academic programs, fostering research excellence, and building sustainable partnerships that accelerate the transition to clean energy solutions.

Our Vision

A Zambia where energy-efficient lighting is mainstream in higher education, driving sustainable development and contributing to global climate goals through locally-adapted innovations and expertise.
